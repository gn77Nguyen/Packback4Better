FAQs

Q: What level of household income qualifies a family for food assistance?
A: There is no requirement to be a part of the program. Backpacks for betters priority is to make sure all families are able to receive proper healthy food and nutrition.
Criteria for Backpacks for Better assistance include:

  • Being a Sponsored Family
  • Qualified for SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)
  • Children in temporary foster care
  • A recommendation from a social worker

    • Q: What's in the regular supplied backpack?
    A: The backpacks are to last a weekend's worth of food per child. So each backpack will consist of a breakfast item, snack, juice, canned lunch, and a dinner item like pasta and canned veggies.
    Q: What if my child has a food allergy?
    A: Meals are available for a variety of dietary requirements. When you contact us or get sponsored by a family, the backpack can be tailored to the participant's needs.
    Q: How "Discrete" is one of these packs?
    A: The backpacks can vary, and styles are selectable given age and gender. The Backpacks are new, donated by local retailers and generous contributors. Selection is limited, but the packs supplied are intended to suit any school.
