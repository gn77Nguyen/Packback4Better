Nutrition and Meal Plan Guides



Meal planning is not prepping like your a high profile chef making a meal for 100 people the day of, meal planning is a fast, cost effective and nutritious way to feed your family and learn about yourself along the way. Creating lists, fun recipes, using coupons, and trying new things are all components of meal planning and they are here to help guide you along your journey to food stability.

Basic Steps to Meal Planning

So how do you meal plan? Do I prep food for the whole weekend, week, month? Well that's up to you, but for beginners let's start with preparing and cooking weekend meals.

Step 1: Research

Plan on finding recipes your family will enjoy but also introduce them to new ingredients and flavors, sites like Pinterest have lots of easy recipes and ways of meal planning. Try to find at least 4-6 recipes since we will be planning for week 1 and another for week 2.

Step 2: Lists and coupons

When creating your list look for what you have in your kitchen that you could use or substitute in the recipe, this limits buying unnecessary things. Then create your grocery list, this should only consist of items needed on the recipes in order to keep you focused and have a goal once in the store. Making a budget for when you go shopping will also help with this.

Step 3: Shopping

Don't get distracted! Items on your list are lst priority, try to stick to it. Remember that budget you created? Look for deals and discounts galore.

Step 4: Prepping

Here we are following the recipes steps and stopping before we cook. This consists of marinating, chopping, and cleaning. Once that's done put all the food away until your ready to cook. The whole idea of prepping is to eliminate the time when you actually ready to cook the food.

Step 5: Cooking! Follow the steps in your recipe for delicious and healthy meals.





Easy Weekend Meal Plans for families of all sizes

Some ways to get your family excited about meal planning are theme nights! Planning fun food nights like Breakfast for Dinner, Pizza night and Taco Night will help make a solid idea of what's being eaten and what needs to be bought. This limits buying food that won't be eaten or roaming the isle looking for something good.

Breakfast for Dinner

Recipe 1:Sausage and Crescent Roll Casserole

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bulk pork sausage
  • 1 tube (8 ounces) refrigerated crescent rolls
  • 2 cups shredded part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups 2% milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375. In a large skillet, cook sausage over medium heat 6-8 minutes or until no longer pink, breaking into crumbles; drain. Unroll crescent roll dough into a greased 13x9-in. baking dish. Seal seams and perforations. Sprinkle with sausage and cheese.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, milk, salt and pepper. Pour over sausage and cheese.
  3. Bake, uncovered, 35-40 minutes or until a knife inserted in the center comes out clean. Let stand 5-10 minutes before serving.

Nutrition Facts:

1 piece: 283 calories, 19g fat (6g saturated fat), 160mg cholesterol, 662mg sodium, 12g carbohydrate (4g sugars, 0 fiber), 15g protein.

Recipe 2: Stuffed Ham and Egg Bread

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons canola oil
  • 1 can (14-1/2 ounces) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 6 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 cups chopped fully cooked ham
  • 1 tube (11 ounces) refrigerated crusty French loaf
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 400. In a large nonstick skillet, heat oil over medium heat. Add tomatoes; cook and stir until juices are evaporated, 12-15 minutes. Add eggs; cook and stir until they are thickened and and no liquid egg remains, 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat; stir in ham.
  2. Unroll dough onto a greased baking sheet. Sprinkle cheese lengthwise down one half of the dough to within 1 in. of edges. Top with egg mixture. Fold dough over filling, pinching to seal; tuck ends under.
  3. Bake until deep golden brown, 17-20 minutes. Cut into slices.

Nutrition Facts:

1 piece: 321 calories, 17g fat (7g saturated fat), 188mg cholesterol, 967mg sodium, 22g carbohydrate (3g sugars, 1g fiber), 22g protein.

Recipe 3:No-Bake Cereal Bars

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups corn syrup
  • 1 jar (40 ounces) chunky peanut butter
  • 6 cups Cheerios oat cereal
  • 6 cups Chex crisp rice cereal

Directions

  1. In a large saucepan, cook and stir sugar and corn syrup until the sugar is dissolved. Remove from the heat. Add peanut butter; mix well. Stir in cereals. Spread quickly into two lightly greased 15x10x1-in. pans. Cut into bars while warm.

Nutrition Facts:

1 each: 95 calories, 5g fat (1g saturated fat), 0 cholesterol, 79mg sodium, 12g carbohydrate (7g sugars, 1g fiber), 3g protein.
